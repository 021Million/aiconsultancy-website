Common questions

Questions I get every week.

What can AI actually automate in a small business?

AI works best on tasks that are repetitive, rules-based, and happen regularly. For a tradie: generating quotes, sending follow-up messages, writing job completion reports, answering common customer questions after hours. For a health or wellness business: client intake forms, appointment reminders, treatment note templates, rebooking sequences. For real estate: property description drafts, buyer follow-up messages, market report summaries.

The rule of thumb: if you have done the same thing three times this week that you have done three hundred times this year, it can almost certainly be automated.

How much does AI automation cost for a small business in NZ?

The tools themselves are typically very affordable — many of the most effective automation tools cost between $0 and $50 a month. My fees depend on the scope of what we build together.

The more useful number to think about: if your time is worth $100 an hour and you are spending ten hours a week on tasks that could be automated, that is $52,000 of your time every year. A well-scoped implementation pays for itself fast.

Is AI going to be complicated to set up for my business?

It should not be. The goal of good implementation is to remove complexity, not add it. A well-designed automation feels simple — because all the complexity is hidden inside it, not exposed to the user.

Most of what I build with clients does not require them to understand how it works — just that it does. Everything comes with plain-English documentation so if something breaks, you know exactly what to do.

Will I be dependent on you to keep things running?

No — and I would not want you to be. Every engagement is designed so you own what gets built, understand how it works, and can use it without ongoing involvement from me. I document everything, train you or your team, and hand over something that runs independently.

Dependency is not in my interest and it is not in yours.

Is my business data safe when using AI tools?

That depends on which tools we use and how we configure them — and I will always explain both before we build anything. I never use your business data to train AI models.

For businesses that handle sensitive client information — most health and real estate businesses — I treat data privacy as a hard constraint, not an afterthought. You will always know what data goes where and why.

I have already tried ChatGPT. Why do I need a consultant?

ChatGPT is a tool — like a drill. Knowing how to use a drill does not tell you where to drill, how deep, or whether you should be drilling at all.

Most business owners find ChatGPT impressive for a week and then go back to their inbox, because they do not have a clear problem defined and a workflow built around it. My job is to identify the specific tasks in your business where AI will make a real difference, build something that fits how you actually operate, and get it running. That is a different thing from a chat window.
