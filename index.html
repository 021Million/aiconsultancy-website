Common questions

Questions we get every week.

What can AI actually automate in a business?

AI works best on tasks that are repetitive, rules-based, and happen regularly. For a tradie: generating quotes, sending follow-up messages, writing job completion reports, answering common customer questions after hours. For a health or wellness business: client intake forms, appointment reminders, treatment note templates, rebooking sequences. For real estate: property description drafts, buyer follow-up messages, market report summaries.

The rule of thumb: if the same task has been done three times this week and three hundred times this year, it can almost certainly be automated.

How much does AI automation cost for a business?

The tools themselves are typically very affordable. Many of the most effective automation tools cost between $0 and $50 a month. Engagement fees depend on the scope of what gets built.

The more useful number to think about: if your time is worth $100 an hour and you are spending ten hours a week on tasks that could be automated, that is $52,000 of your time every year. A well-scoped implementation pays for itself fast.

Is AI going to be complicated to set up for my business?

It should not be. The goal of good implementation is to remove complexity, not add it. A well-designed automation feels simple, because all the complexity is hidden inside it, not exposed to the user.

Most of what we build does not require clients to understand how it works, just that it does. Everything comes with plain-English documentation so if something breaks, you know exactly what to do.

Will we be dependent on AI Consultancy to keep things running?

No. Every engagement is designed so you own what gets built, understand how it works, and can use it without ongoing involvement. We document everything, train your team, and hand over something that runs independently.

Dependency is not in our interest and it is not in yours.

Is my business data safe when using AI tools?

That depends on which tools are used and how they are configured, and this is always explained before anything is built. Your business data is never used to train AI models.

For businesses that handle sensitive client information, data privacy is treated as a hard constraint, not an afterthought. You will always know what data goes where and why.

I have already tried ChatGPT. Why do I need a consultant?

ChatGPT is a tool, like a drill. Knowing how to use a drill does not tell you where to drill, how deep, or whether you should be drilling at all.

Most business owners find ChatGPT impressive for a week and then go back to their inbox, because they do not have a clear problem defined and a workflow built around it. The job of an AI consultant is to identify the specific tasks in your business where AI will make a real difference, build something that fits how you actually operate, and get it running. That is a different thing from a chat window.
